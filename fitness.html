FITNESS

WORKOUT PLAN

Creating a workout plan that fits your needs is the first step in fitness. A good workout plan challenges your level of fitness and helps you lose or maintain weight. Your workout plan should include a cardio component, a weights component, a stretching component, and a cool down period.

Working out can improve your mood!

Not only does working out improve your physical health by lowering your chances of disease, it also helps imrove your mental health. Working out releases endorphins into your system improving your mood. Improved mood and a better self image are key parts in your mental health.

Working out improves physical health!

Working out burns off excess calories that your body consumed. It also burns fat and prevents some diseases. People who workout regularily visit the doctor less because their immune systems are stronger.

SETTING GOALS

Setting small goals in your workout routine can help you stay motivated. Try seting goals that are easily achievable.

Cardio

The first component of a good workout is the cardio. You should find an activity that you enjoy so that you'll want to do it. Swimming, running, and biking are three of the most common cardio choices.

Weights/Resistance Training

Weights training helps improve muscle strength, increase muscular indurance, and to maintain bone density. Weights training is to tone your muscles, making you even leaner.

Flexibility Exercises

Stretching helps increase flexibility, improve range of motion, and keep your muscles from cramping up. Stretching should be done at the begining and end of every workout. It helps you mentally prepare for the workout as well as keep your body limber.

Cool Down

A cool down period consists of stretching and returning your heart rate and breathing down to normal. Its important to stretch and walk after a workout so you muscles don't obtain lactic acid buildup.

TIPS

Working out is the second component in maintaining a healthy lifestyle. It improves mood, immune sytem functions, and physical health.

Tunes

An awesome playlist can be supe motivating. Spotify has playlists to run, squat, and lift to. Or you could make your own rockin playlist.

Phone a Friend

Staying motivated is hard to do alone, so finding a gym buddy is key.

Sore

After a workout you're going to be sore, a remedy to this is a cold bath.

Perfect Outfit

Find the right shoes and outfit to wear during your workout. If you've decided to start running, get some running shoes and some sweat wicking clothes.

Weigh-ins

Check your weight in the morning when you don't have a fully belly. Keeping track of your weight can also motivate you to work harder.

Water

Remember to keep hydrate. Drinking water throughout your workout will help maintain body temp and keep you from collapsing.
